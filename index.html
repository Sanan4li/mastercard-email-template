A Decade of Impact: Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program Celebrates 10 Years at KNUST

Celebrating a decade defined by transformative leadership, inclusive education, and community impact, the Kwame Nkrumah University of Science and Technology (KNUST) in Kumasi, Ghana and the Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program commemorated 10 years of partnership this June.

The Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program at KNUST has supported 750 Mastercard Foundation Scholars in its first phase and is on track to support 1,500 more, with a focus on recruiting 70 percent young women.

The three-day celebrations were highlighted by a community transformation project at Ayigya Basic School. A group of Mastercard Foundation Scholars renovated classrooms, improved the ICT lab, built a mechanized borehole, and constructed a wall around the school, aiming to enhance student safety, hygiene, access to clean water, and digital literacy.

Life of Impact Volunteering Projects Recognised as Changemakers

The Life of Impact Volunteering Initiative, led by Inga Ackermann, Online Learning and Leadership Coordinator for the Mastercard Foundation Scholars Program, was awarded the prestigious Change maker Award by the University of Edinburgh in May 2025. The initiative mobilized 75 Africa-based online Mastercard Foundation Scholars across ten African countries, the USA, Canada, and the UK, to implement high-impact, community-based volunteer projects.

Supported by the Scottish Funding Council International Science Partnership Fund, the initiative empowered students through micro-grants and peer-led coordination to deliver socially responsible, sustainable interventions. Each student retained ownership of their project while receiving light-touch guidance from four peer coordinators, proving that even modest investments can drive profound community change.

Reeta Roy Receives Ashesi Medal for Transformative Leadership

Reeta Roy , President and CEO of the Mastercard Foundation, was awarded the inaugural Ashesi Medal on June 5, 2025, during Ashesi University’ s Commencement Ceremony . The Medal, Ashesi’ s highest honor is conferred by the Board of Directors to individuals whose lives exemplify the values of scholarship, leadership, and citizenship, and who have made significant contributions to Africa through philanthropy or service.

Roy’ s leadership at the Mastercard Foundation since 2008 has been instrumental in advancing education, public health, and economic empowerment across Africa. Her vision led to transformative programs such as the “Saving Lives and Livelihoods” initiative with the Africa CDC, which increased COVID-19 adult vaccination rates in Africa from 3% to 53%, while training and deploying 40,000 health workers.

Under her stewardship:
  • Over 55 million youth have received work-enabling skills.
  • Nearly 15 million young people have secured employment.
  • The Mastercard Foundation has formed pioneering education partnerships, including with Ashesi University.

Roy’ s decision to remain in Africa during the pandemic and her deep commitment to grassroots leadership have set a new standard in philanthropy .
